Photos are nice. I still remember film and video tapes from my early childhood. We owned many video cameras and point-and-shoot and compacts. I remember using one (and losing one, unfortunately), but I wasn't really walking around taking pictures like I am now. This is because I was 7.

A bit later, I got into smartphone. I had a camera that's always on me, so I started taking pictures. At first, of the people around me engaging in various activities. But then people failed me, so I started to shoot nature instead. Moving in Canada means I had a lot of opportunity to explore nature. For example, I've taken great photos of sunsets at Muskoka.

I once scoffed at the idea of owning a dedicated camera. It was hard to imagine that it could take better photos and be as easy to use as my phone. Back then, DSLRs were mainstream, and mirrorless cameras were only begin to popularize. DSLRs are heavy and clunky. I used one during a media class in high school. Wasn't a fan of the camera at all, but this planted a seed for the interest in photography.
